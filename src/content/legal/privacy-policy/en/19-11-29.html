Installation, operation and rejection of cookies

The company does not use cookies in a way that stores user personal information. The cookies used by the company are only for maintaining member login and single sign-on between family sites. Cookies are small text files that are sent to the user’s computer by the server used to run the company's website and stored on the user’s computer's hard disk. The user has the option of installing cookies or choosing not to use them.

How to decline cookie settings (Example: in Internet Explorer): At the top of your web browser, click Tools > Internet Options > Privacy tab. Under settings, move the slider to the top to block all cookies or to the bottom to allow all cookies. Then click Apply. You can either accept or check each time you save a cookie, or you can refuse to save all cookies. However, if you refuse to store cookies, there will be restrictions on the use of some services that require login.

Technological and administrative protection measures of personal information

To protect user personal information from being lost, stolen, leaked, altered or damaged, the Company shall take the following technical and administrative measures. The Company takes comprehensive measures according to laws and regulations.

  • Password Encryption: User password is encrypted, stored and managed. In principle, only the user can know the password. Only the user who knows the password can view and modify personal information.
  • Countermeasures against hacking, Computer Viruses: The Company will do its best to prevent personal information from being leaked or damaged by hacking or computer viruses. By using the latest antivirus program, the Company prevents personal information from being leaked and protect personal information completely through encryption communication when transmitting personal information. The Company uses intrusion prevention system to control unauthorized access from the outside and strives to have all possible technical equipment to ensure even better security.
  • Minimize processing staff and Conduct security training: The Company limits the number of employees who process personal information to a minimum and conducts training for relevant employees on a regular basis to confirm compliance with the personal information processing policy and compliance.
